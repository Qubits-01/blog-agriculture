The Harmful Effects of Using Pesticides.
Is It Really Worth It?
Randa Ancheta   August 24, 2019

   Pesticide poisoning is an increasing problem in the Philippines. It’s not just a problem that’s common in adult farmers alone, but it’s also common for school children who are working at a young age in vegetable plots. They are exposed to the chemicals present in pesticides because they are also the ones primarily responsible in the manual preparation of such products. As their dependence on pesticide usage increases, so are the traces found in soil and rivers nearby the agricultural fields. Pesticide traces may affect the health of decomposers and aquatic beings present.

   We can actually use insects as our allies instead of pests. A simple solution could be nurturing beehives next to agricultural land. While its population has greatly decreased in the United States, which called for the creation of drones to carry out their role in agriculture, bees are still relatively abundant in the Philippines. Bees are very helpful for pollination. Plus, farmers can have an extra source of income – honey. The use of artificial products is already decreased by this measure. To complete it, we can also ask for the aid of insect predators, specifically those that eat agricultural pests. Several species of different kinds are available in the country.
