Inflation - Food Prices Increasing,
What Can We Do?
Randa Ancheta   August 6, 2019

   In 2007-2008, as world prices peaked (rising by as much as 87%), global food stocks plummeted to levels not seen in two decades. Coupled with the global economic slowdown, surging food prices pushed an estimated 100 million more people into hunger, raising the total number of undernourished people globally to over 1 billion.

   ADB economists predict that a 10% rise in domestic food price inflation in developing Asia could push 64 million more Asians into poverty. These unprecedented global food price surges expose vulnerabilities to food and nutrition insecurity not only for the region's households but also of its governments.

   This prediction can be overturned by always keeping in mind that sustainability is the key. With all the technological advancements, solutions, and insights listed in this blog, we still have the chance to change things. We can give the farmers more opportunities and make trade more flexible. We can lessen the unnecessary labor with sustainable solutions that will last. We can still break the stigma that farming and agriculture are made for the poor, because it really isn't. It's a practice that should be appreciated by everybody, whether one plans to make a small vegetable plot or a hacienda. With agriculture, we can have a greener environment, a future of sustainability, and healthier and longer lives.
