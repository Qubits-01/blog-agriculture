Farm to Market Roads.
A Backbone for Agriculture
Randa Ancheta   October 12, 2019

   The Philippines is rich in land, so it’s only but usual to set up agricultural lands. In many cases though, agricultural lands are very far from the markets or civilization in general. Travel time for local exportation takes too long, and aside from distance, another common factor for this are non-cemented and hard-to-pass-through roads. Products may get spoiled during local exportation itself. This may then result to an increase in prices of fruits, vegetables, and any other commodity which would need raw products. There’s also a chance for bankruptcy for the farmers.

   The problem asks for administrative solutions, such as the construction of better roads, provision of power, and development of market infrastructure such as rural growth centers. Someone should be responsible for the facilitation of agribusiness and food trade. While trade is straightforward in the country, it’s still unrefined. It would help if all channels of delivery have quality control centers so for every checkpoint, products are sure to get the proper maintenance.
